Vaibhavi Merchant’s musical showcase, The Merchants of Bollywood has been stunning the European audiences for the past three years. Vaibhavi says that the musical has been loosely based on her own life. "It is a colourful and dramatic musical tale of a young woman, who rebels against her grandfather, who has been a successful choreographer and choreographed the likes of Raj Kapoor and Shammi Kapoor, during his times. It is the

 
 

clash of traditions and thinking between the the clash of traditions and thinking between the two dancers," she says. The Merchants of Bollywood has already successfully toured Australia, New Zealand, UK and the United States in the last four years, and is internationally touted as the "first authentic musical from Bollywood ". "NRIs were tired of watching star shows. The westerners are tired of watching the musicals. The Merchants of Bollywood was something new. It brought alive the romance and drama of Indian films, with a burst of colour and larger-than-life experience of the music and famous stars from our legendary industry. In the process, I got to learn a lot like the sheer value of stage production. How a show opens and how stars come out. Their lighting and costumes. We consciously made an effort to keep the audience in mind," says Vaibhavi.

She adds, "I was asked to perform but I am very shy and don’t like to come on stage and there’s so much to do backstage. I have my own troupe and am handling 40 dancers at a time."

The Merchants of Bollywood has travelled all over the world, but . has not yet played in India. "We couldn’t find the right venue to stage a show like this. We zeroed down a place, but later realised it was not comfortable. We are looking out for options but maybe later sometime," she concludes.
