Miss Bollywood in trouble? (Times of India – Indiatiemes.com)

 
  October 16, 2007  

..............................................................................................................................................................
 
   

Vaibhavi Merchant and producer Mark Brady of the famed all-Indian touring musical, The Merchants of Bollywood, are not too happy with the uncanny similarity that Shilpa Shetty’s musical, Miss Bollywood, bears to their three-year-old production. According to them, Miss Bollywood which opened in Germany last week, is inspired from The Merchants of Bollywood.

 
 


Vaibhavi says, “Mohammed Morani (organiser and producer of Miss Bollywood) had seen our shows and they couldn’t believe the reactions we received. I’m sure they felt that they could do it as well.

The storylines: The Merchants of Bollywood traces the journey of Ayesha who rebels against her family of classical dancers to make a foray into Bollywood. Miss Bollywood revolves around this young choreographer called Maya (played by Shilpa) who is trained in the classical dances and who struggles to popularise the Bollywood brand of dancing in the UK. “The only difference is that the show organisers, Morani brothers, have cleverly roped in Shilpa Shetty. In our case, the narrative is the USP,” informs Mark, producer of The Merchants of Bollywood.

Mohammed Morani, one of the organisers and producers of Miss Bollywood rules out any similarity between the two theatrical productions. He says, “How can they accuse us of lifting their plot? Our storyline is completely different from theirs. The Morani Brothers are pioneers in the show business. We don’t need to resort to such means.”

What is interesting is that both the musicals are touring during the same time and the competition is too imminent. While Miss Bollywood has already begun its tour across Europe, The Merchants of Bollywood will open its third leg this November. However, Vaibhavi is not afraid of competition. She says, “We are three years old in the business and that works as an advantage.”
