  Sultans of Bollywood (After Hrs DNA)  
  October 13, 2007  
..............................................................................................................................................................
 
     

With an eclectic mix of projects lined up, music composers Salim and Sulaiman Merchant are living it up!
The duo is flying high, what with their latest track ‘Chak De’ turning into an anthem the whole country is humming and a string of Bollywood films they’ve worked for waiting to be released! Plus, of course,

 
 

the international collaborations and experimentations keeping them pinned as the sultans of Indian music at the global level.

Duo Salim and Sulaiman Merchant are a lot of fun; they have dope on all the latest happenings in the music industry. “The one thing we feel really sad about is music composers ripping off someone else’s music. It’s a shame,” fumes Salim.

They are most excited about a 25 -minute opera piece that is being composed for ‘Aaja Nach Le’. “It’s an experience in itself. It’s presented in a western life and we believe will look great on screen,” reveals Salim. Their ‘Merchants of Bollywood 2008’ is another project that the brothers will be touring for. “This is the follow up to the first one and it’s our pleasure to present to the west what our culture is,” he says.

According to them, something like ‘Merchants of Bollywood’ in terms of concept will not work in India for the simple reason that theatre here is not big enough. “Besides the whole concept is developed for the west to have a peek into our culture,” says Salim.

Sulaiman lists out the number of projects lined up for them: ‘Aaja Nach Le’, ‘Roadside Romeo’, ‘Coochie Coochie Hota Hai’, ‘Aashayein’ and ‘Bombay to Bangok’. So, who is their favourite director? “We like all of them, but Nagesh is one of our favourites. It’s very chilled out to just hang around with him and come up with great music,” smiles Sulaiman.

That reminds Salim of his collaborations with Anoushka Shankar and Karsh Kale. Their album ‘Breathing Underwater’ which has just been released internationally features Salim. “It was great collaborating with Anoushka. I was touring with her and Karsh and it was a lot of fun. After the performances we would just hang out, listen to music through the night, everything from Mehdi Hassan to Infected Mushrooms,” he laughs.
